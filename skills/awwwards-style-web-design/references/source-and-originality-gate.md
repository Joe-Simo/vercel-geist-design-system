## Source And Originality Gate

This skill is based on independently written, high-level synthesis of public
experiential web-design patterns and public design-award evaluation concepts.
It does not bundle or require third-party content. You may inspect public
benchmark sites only for brief, high-level calibration of abstract craft
principles. Do not mine, copy, closely paraphrase, embed, scrape, summarize in
detail, or redistribute third-party screenshots, page text, source code, assets,
profile metadata, voting data, or archive material. Use only official public
facts for factual claims and user-provided, rights-cleared assets for
implementation. Live facts, award status, legal terms, and current references
must be verified against official sources when they matter.

Required stance:

- Independent community skill, not official Awwwards guidance.
- Original design synthesis, not a clone generator.
- Public Awwwards references are inspiration inputs, not reusable templates.
- Awwwards scoring dimensions are useful critique lenses, not a promise of
  official scoring, nomination, or award outcome.
- This is a skill, not a plugin, template installer, starter-kit selector, or
  boilerplate cloning workflow.

Benchmark calibration gate:

- Use 3-5 benchmark references only to calibrate ambition, not to borrow
  identity.
- Prefer official/live sources when current award status, winner names, or legal
  terms matter.
- For each benchmark, extract only abstract principles: concept clarity,
  first-viewport impact, interaction category, motion discipline, content
  specificity, responsive craft, and technical polish. Do not capture exact
  layouts, copy, frame sequences, asset treatments, or distinctive art direction.
- Compare the planned work against the benchmarks by dimension, then name the
  weakest dimension before implementation.
- If the direction looks recognizable as any benchmark, stop and redesign the
  concept.

When using references, build a reference matrix before designing:

| Reference | Why It Applies | Abstract Principle | What Must Not Be Copied | Project-Specific Translation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| URL/name | Surface, audience, mechanic, or craft reason | Concept, structure, motion, content, or technical lesson | Layout, brand, copy, assets, art direction, animation sequence, trade dress | Original decision tied to this project |

Never justify a design with "that winning site did it." Translate references
into project-specific principles: content-native interaction, scene structure,
art-direction discipline, motion grammar, asset strategy, responsive behavior,
and technical craft.

Originality check before final output:

- The result is not recognizable as a clone of any named winner or template.
- It does not use winner/client logos, protected characters, screenshots,
  proprietary copy, distinctive campaign concepts, or trade dress.
- It combines abstract principles from multiple references with the user's own
  domain, audience, content, constraints, and brand direction.
- Any reference to a winner is analytical and brief.
- It does not imply Awwwards affiliation, endorsement, submission, nomination,
  or award status.
- It does not copy from templates, demo apps, starter kits, boilerplates,
  unofficial source code, or generated template snippets.

Asset, consent, and privacy guardrail:

- Do not ingest, retain, rank, profile, enrich, contact, expose, or make claims
  about individual jurors, voters, members, submitters, agency staff, or other
  people from award pages, profile pages, voting metadata, directories, or
  third-party archives.
- If attribution is needed, cite the public page URL/title and the
  design-relevant entity only; do not reproduce person-level rows or contact
  details.
- Verify rights and consent for identifiable people, user-submitted content,
  location-sensitive media, private spaces, client/product data, profile data,
  testimonials, quotes, photography, video, audio, scans, 3D captures, and model
  releases before using them.
- Do not use scraping, enrichment, or profiling to create design substance.
- Prefer aggregate judging patterns and design criteria.

Safety and integrity guardrail:

- Do not help build deceptive, phishing, scam, impersonation,
  credential-harvesting, illegal, hateful, sexually exploitative, or violent
  experiences.
- Do not recommend dark patterns, fake awards, fake credibility, inaccessible
  spectacle, or privacy-invasive tracking.
- Preserve honest product claims, consent, accessibility, reduced-motion
  support, semantic HTML, and performance even when pursuing high visual impact.

If official or live sources cannot be reached, say what was attempted and label
the work `local inspiration, not source-verified`.
